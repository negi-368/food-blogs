Cookie Policy

Last updated: July 27, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how Food Blog ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies to recognize you when you visit our website. It explains what these technologies are and why we use them, as well as your rights to control our use of them.

What are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make their websites work, or to work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information.

Why Do We Use Cookies?

We use cookies for several reasons:

  • Essential cookies: Some cookies are essential for the operation of our website
  • Performance cookies: These help us understand how visitors interact with our recipes and content
  • Functionality cookies: These enable enhanced functionality like saving your favorite recipes
  • Analytics cookies: These help us analyze how our food blog is being used

Types of Cookies We Use

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility. Without these cookies, services like recipe saving cannot be provided.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our food blog, such as which recipes are viewed most often and if visitors encounter any issues. This information helps us improve our content and user experience.

Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as dietary preferences or favorite cuisines) and provide enhanced, more personal features like personalized recipe recommendations.

Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that place cookies on your device. These include:

  • Google Analytics for website performance analysis
  • Social media platforms for sharing recipes and content
  • Advertising networks to show relevant food-related ads
  • Commenting systems to facilitate discussions about recipes

How Long Do Cookies Stay on My Device?

The length of time a cookie stays on your device depends on whether it is a "persistent" or "session" cookie:

  • Session cookies: These are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies: These remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them

How to Control Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. You can:

  • View what cookies you have and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from particular sites
  • Block all cookies from being set
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Browser-Specific Instructions

For more information on how to manage cookies in specific browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies
  • Firefox: Preferences > Privacy & Security > Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies
  • Edge: Settings > Site permissions > Cookies

Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable cookies, some features of our food blog may not function properly. Specifically:

  • You may need to re-enter information more frequently
  • Personalized recipe recommendations won't work
  • We may not be able to remember your dietary preferences
  • Recipe saving functionality may be limited

Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other tracking technologies such as:

  • Web beacons: Small graphic images that track whether you have viewed a particular recipe
  • Pixels: Small pieces of code that can track user behavior and recipe conversions
  • Local storage: A way for our website to store recipe data locally within your browser

Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. Please revisit this Cookie Policy regularly to stay informed about our use of cookies.

Contact Us

If you have any questions about our use of cookies or this Cookie Policy, please contact us.
